The Premise: Studying Light, Not Faking Art

A psychological horror/romance of ego and obsession where endings span symbiosis, codependency, redemption, or destruction. Choose to save or damn your stalker—but are you qualified to judge when their drive was simply companionship?

Built by math/systems training, not art school. Artists like Caravaggio, Fra Angelico, El Greco, and Holbein become dissected problems: light as weaponized attention, shadow as moral ambiguity.

Philosophy as Moral Trap

Eroticism, Death, and Sensuality frames stalker/stalkee codependency: obsession becomes mutual imprisonment. Nietzsche's amor fati versus Baudrillard's simulation—can you accept fate when reality feels co-authored?

Jungian shadow work through tailoring reveals ego fractures. Visions decay from devotional serenity to grotesque corruption. Existentialism asks: what is right when companionship and predation wear the same face?

Q.E.D.: Tenebrism reveals what morality conceals in shadow.

[Tenebrism Note] Extreme Baroque lighting where darkness dominates and a single light source spotlights key elements. Here: shadows = unasked questions; light = moral choices forced into view.

Reverse-Engineering Method

Math-trained dissection of classical light/shadow grammars and horror geometry constraints. Blender blockouts → Unity URP rigs where unlit surfaces = unasked moral questions about codependence versus freedom.

Visual Pathology

Tenebrist light carves ego masks from low-poly flesh. Devotional visions corrupt into Dorian Gray pathology as obsession metastasizes. Darkness conceals neither geometry nor the shared delusion binding captor and captive.

Tailoring exposes what faces and light conceal: the wound demanding witness, regardless of consequence.
