The Premise: Studying Light, Not Faking Art

A psychological thriller about power dynamics and moral ambiguity. Choose to save or damn your stalker—but are you qualified to judge when their drive was simply companionship?

Built by math/systems training, not art school. Artists like Caravaggio, Fra Angelico, El Greco, and Holbein become dissected problems: light as weaponized attention, shadow as moral ambiguity.

Philosophy as Moral Trap

Machiavelli's power inversion + Nietzsche's amor fati versus Baudrillard's simulation—can you accept fate when reality feels co-authored? Captor becomes captive; control reveals itself as mutual delusion.

Jungian shadow work through tailoring reveals Pierrot/Harlequin ego fractures: the sad-clown masks we wear for love. Thanatos (death drive) powers vision corruption from devotional serenity to grotesque decay. Existentialism asks: what is right when companionship and predation wear the same face?
