WGU — How It Actually Works
Competency Units (CU) vs. GPAWGU does not use GPA. No letter grades. You demonstrate competency and pass, or you continue until you do. Each course carries Competency Units — analogous to credit hours. 120 CU to graduate; 79 already satisfied by prior credentials. The remaining 41 CU are the active curriculum on this calendar.
Minimum requirement per termEach term is six months, flat tuition. You must complete at least one CU per term to remain enrolled. No maximum — students with prior background routinely finish a full term's worth of courses in 6–10 weeks. Acceleration is financially rewarded: fewer terms = lower total cost.
Proctored exams — what students don't expectMost WGU assessments are performance tasks graded by evaluators. Some courses have proctored objective assessments — timed, multiple-choice, browser-proctored. These require a private room, webcam covering face and workspace, and government ID. An external webcam is strongly recommended — built-in cameras are often too narrow and trigger flags. Test setup before exam day, not on it.
Common misconceptionsOnline does not mean open book for proctored exams. Proctoring software monitors screen, webcam, and microphone throughout. Performance tasks are open-resource but evaluated on demonstrated understanding — AI-generated or copied submissions fail. Retake cooldown periods exist. First-attempt passing is worth optimizing for.
NeetCode — Why It's in This Calendar
What NeetCode isNeetCode (neetcode.io) is a structured interview prep resource built around the NeetCode 150 — organized by pattern, not difficulty. Where LeetCode is 2500+ problems, NeetCode is an opinionated subset with video explanations covering the 15 core patterns that appear in the vast majority of SWE interviews.
The 15 core patternsArrays & Hashing · Two Pointers · Sliding Window · Stack · Binary Search · Linked List · Trees · Tries · Heap / Priority Queue · Backtracking · Graphs · Advanced Graphs · 1D Dynamic Programming · 2D Dynamic Programming · Bit Manipulation. Introduced here in roadmap order, synchronized with LeetCode problems each week.
How to use it alongside LeetCodeAttempt the problem independently first. Watch the NeetCode video only if you're stuck — not as a substitute for thinking. Goal: pattern recognition, not memorization. When you can solve a new problem in a familiar pattern without looking up the approach, the pattern has landed.
Roadmap vs. NeetCode 150The roadmap (neetcode.io/roadmap) shows all 150 problems by pattern with difficulty — start here. For Phases 1–2 you're covering Arrays & Hashing, Two Pointers, and Sliding Window — roughly the first 30 roadmap problems. Do not skip ahead. Pattern depth beats problem count at this stage.
